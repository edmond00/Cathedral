# Body Parts and Their Functions

## Physical Body Parts

### Lower Limbs
Governs gross leg movement and locomotion. Primary body part for running, jumping, sprinting, and evasive maneuvers. Essential for mobility and physical displacement.

### Upper Limbs
Controls arm and hand strength for forceful actions. Responsible for pushing, lifting, striking, pulling, and wrestling. The body's primary tool for exerting physical force.

### Feet
Manages balance, quiet movement, and spatial awareness while moving. Governs stealth, scouting, skulking, and maintaining equilibrium. The foundation of graceful and subtle locomotion.

### Fingers
Controls fine motor skills and intricate manual work. Essential for crafting, lockpicking, etching, sewing, and carving. The seat of precision and delicate manipulation.

### Backbone
Represents physical stability, posture, and core strength. Governs composure, equilibrium, posture, stance, and alignment. The structural foundation of physical control.

## Vital Organs

### Thorax
The seat of vocal projection and breath control. Powers singing, shouting, chanting, bellowing, and reciting. The lungs' capacity for sustained vocalization and expression.

### Viscera
Represents physical endurance and internal fortitude. Governs stamina, endurance, resistance to hardship, and digestive health. The body's capacity to endure strain and deprivation.

### Heart
The emotional center and source of passion. Controls charm, empathy, courage, passion, and resolve. Drives emotional strength and interpersonal connection.

## Sensory Organs

### Ears
Governs auditory perception and musical ability. Essential for music, opera, hearing, whispering, and detecting sounds. The gateway of sound and rhythm.

### Eyes
Controls visual perception and observation. Responsible for reading, spotting, scanning, aiming, and detailed observation. The primary tool for gathering visual information.

### Tongue
The instrument of speech and persuasion. Governs speaking, persuasion, bargaining, deception, and threatening. The body's tool for verbal communication and influence.

### Nose
Manages olfactory senses and scent-based skills. Essential for cooking, tracking, smelling, foraging, and sniffing. The organ of scent discrimination and chemical detection.

## Brain Regions

### Cerebrum
The seat of higher reasoning and analytical thought. Governs mathematics, logic, rhetoric, analysis, and planning. The engine of rational thought and strategic thinking.

### Cerebellum
Controls motor coordination and complex physical sequences. Essential for dancing, crafting, juggling, balancing, and riding. The coordinator of practiced physical movements.

### Anamnesis
The repository of memory and historical knowledge. Governs history lore, recollection, memorizing, remembering, and recording. The library of past experiences and learned information.

### Hippocampus
The wellspring of creativity and artistic expression. Controls poetry, theater, dreaming, designing, and inventing. The source of imagination and creative synthesis.

### Pineal Gland
The seat of contemplation and inner wisdom. Governs philosophy, intuition, meditating, reflecting, and pondering. The organ of introspection and deeper understanding.
